Melbourne's public transport is an essential way for people to travel and live in the city. Trams, trains and buses make up an excellent public transport network.

Tram Overview

city circle stop tram tracks

The map contains all tram tracks within the city of Melbourne. The Melbourne tram is the second most used form of public transport in Melbourne, and the world’s largest tram network with nearly 500 trams on 24 routes, and 1763 tram stops. Providing services to almost 3 million passenger each year.

City Circle tram service

city circle stop city circle route
Click to see more info

The most popular route – City Circle Tram (route 35) is a free and Convenient way to see the major attractions of central Melbourne, operating in a circular route and linking with other tram, trains and bus routes in and around Melbourne.

It includes 28 stops, covering most of the travel needs of people living and working in Melbourne's CBD.

People have mixed feelings about the cycling experience in Melbourne

bike share dock bicycle routes

Those who had ridden at least once in the past year were asked about their perceptions of riding in the City of Melbourne. Most of those who had ridden indicated they felt comfortable (53%) or very comfortable (20%) doing so. Most riders felt that conditions for riding in the City of Melbourne had remained about the same (47%) and were better (42%) over the last 12 months.
